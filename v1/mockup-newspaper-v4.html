Meet the Engineer Building Tomorrow's Web

From competitive programming in high school to shipping production code at Silicon Valley's top companies—the story of a builder who never stops.

YK

Yash Kothari, Software Engineer — San Francisco, 2025

Yash Kothari doesn't just write code—he ships products that people actually use. Currently building at Vercel, the platform powering the modern web, he's part of the team making deployment as simple as a git push.

A Computer Science student at the University of Waterloo (class of 2026), Kothari has already completed five internships at companies ranging from early-stage startups to industry giants. His work has impacted thousands of users across 70+ countries.

"I enjoy building products that help people," he says. "There's something deeply satisfying about solving a hard problem and watching real people benefit from the solution."

His journey has taken him through Shopify's security organization (where he won an internal hackathon), SeatGeek's catalog team, and the crypto frontier at Nest Wallet—each stop adding new tools to an already impressive repertoire.
