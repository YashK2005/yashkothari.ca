Breaking News from Silicon Valley

Local Engineer Joins Vercel,
Vows to "Ship Fast and Break Nothing"

From the halls of UWaterloo to the forefront of web infrastructure, one developer's relentless pursuit of elegant solutions

Special Correspondent • Technology Desk

YK

Mr. Kothari, photographed at his workstation

In a move that has sent ripples through the technology sector, Yash Kothari, a promising young engineer from the University of Waterloo, has accepted a position at Vercel, the renowned web deployment platform.

The appointment marks the latest chapter in what observers are calling a "meteoric rise" through the ranks of Silicon Valley's most prestigious firms.

Previous Tenure at SeatGeek Yields Remarkable Results

During his time with the Catalog team at SeatGeek, Mr. Kothari distinguished himself through his work scaling the Seat Perks program to venues nationwide.

"The man simply ships," remarked one colleague, speaking on condition of anonymity. "Baseball games, Broadway shows—he experienced them all while somehow delivering exceptional code."

"I enjoy building products that help people and working on difficult problems."

Shopify Security Triumph: AI Platform Wins Hackathon

The Security organization at Shopify witnessed something extraordinary when Mr. Kothari led development of an AI-powered intelligence platform featuring semantic search and RAG capabilities.

The project earned top honors at an internal competition, cementing his reputation as an innovator.

Running Journal Application Reaches Global Audience

In perhaps his most personal venture, an iOS application for tracking running workouts has attracted over 1,000 athletes across 70 countries.

Built with Swift and SwiftUI, the application represents Mr. Kothari's commitment to helping people achieve their fitness goals.
